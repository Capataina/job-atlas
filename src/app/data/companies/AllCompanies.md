# Company Tiers

> Curated, evergreen company tiers to support a low-entropy, high-leverage job search. Use this to stay focused on durable targets with strong engineering, visa capability, and long-term career signal—rather than reacting to every posting.

---

## How to Use This List

- Start with Tier 1, then Tier 2; Tier 3 is for expanding scope without sacrificing signal.
- Tier 4 (recruiters) is for selective reach-outs; they are not direct employers.
- Loop through a small set consistently (roles, referrals, interview prep) instead of mass-applying.
- Verify visa sponsorship and London presence before investing significant effort.
- Treat this as a living document—company quality and hiring patterns shift.

---

## Tier Definitions

### Tier 1 — Global Apex

_The name alone compounds forever._

| Criterion               | Threshold                                                                                |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Recognition**         | Instant global recognition across industries. Explaining where you work is never needed. |
| **Engineering Density** | Working alongside genuinely elite practitioners is the default, not the exception.       |
| **Selectivity**         | Acceptance rates comparable to top universities (<5% for most roles).                    |
| **Compensation**        | Top-decile or higher. Total comp meaningfully above market.                              |
| **Visa**                | Sponsorship is routine/automatic—built into hiring process.                              |
| **Problems**            | Frontier-defining. Setting industry direction, not following it.                         |
| **Career Half-Life**    | Infinite. Twenty years later, it still opens doors.                                      |

**Archetypes:** Google, Jane Street, OpenAI, NVIDIA, DeepMind

---

### Tier 2 — Global Elite

_Domain leaders with strong but bounded signal._

| Criterion               | Threshold                                                                                |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Recognition**         | Strong recognition within tech/finance; may require brief explanation outside industry.  |
| **Engineering Density** | Excellent engineers, though concentration may be team-dependent.                         |
| **Selectivity**         | Very competitive (5–15% acceptance for technical roles).                                 |
| **Compensation**        | Top-quartile. Competitive with Tier 1 in some cases.                                     |
| **Visa**                | Available but may require more navigation; company-dependent.                            |
| **Problems**            | Important, often best-in-class within domain. Not always frontier.                       |
| **Career Half-Life**    | 5–10 years of strong signal. Excellent stepping stone to Tier 1 or leadership elsewhere. |

**Archetypes:** Databricks, Stripe, ARM, Cohere, Goldman Sachs (Strats), Flow Traders

---

### Tier 3 — Strong Signal / Serious Engineering

_Real problems, good learning, credible credential._

| Criterion               | Threshold                                                                 |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Recognition**         | Known within industry/domain. Outside tech, may need explanation.         |
| **Engineering Density** | Good culture, serious technical work. Quality varies by team.             |
| **Selectivity**         | Competitive but achievable (15–30% for target roles).                     |
| **Compensation**        | Market rate to moderately above. Equity upside possible at earlier-stage. |
| **Visa**                | Possible but inconsistent. Requires explicit verification.                |
| **Problems**            | Real and interesting. Good skill-building environment.                    |
| **Career Half-Life**    | 3–5 years. Good for building experience, less for long-term brand.        |

**Archetypes:** Grafana Labs, Monzo, Wayve, QuestDB, InstaDeep

---

### Tier 4 — Recruiters & Talent Intermediaries

_Access layer, not destination._

| Criterion            | Threshold                                                                                                |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Value**            | Can surface roles not on public boards; relationships with hiring managers.                              |
| **Risk**             | Quality variance is extreme. Many will waste time with irrelevant roles.                                 |
| **Engagement Model** | Selective. Engage specialists aligned with your target domains (quant, ML, systems). Ignore generalists. |

**Archetypes:** Oxford Knight (quant), Selby Jennings (finance), IC Resources (hardware)
